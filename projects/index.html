Eugene Torre

The best player in Philippine Chess history.

Here's a timeline of Eugene Torre's life:

  • 1951 - Born in Iloilo, Philippines
  • 1972 - Asia's first grandmaster at 22
  • 1969 - World Junior Chess Championship, Champion Final B
  • 1971 - World Junior Chess Championship, 4th place
  • 1972 - Asian Zonal Championships, Champion and becomes Philippines' 3rd International Master (IM)
  • 1976 - The Marlboro-Loyola Kings Challenge, Champion (becoming the first player to finish ahead of Anatoly Karpov since Karpov became world champion)
  • 1977 - Asian Chess Team Championships, Gold Medal on Board 1
  • 1979 - 7th Marlboro Classic, Champion
  • 1979 - Asian Chess Team Championships, Gold Medal on Board
  • 1980 - La Valletta, Malta Chess Olympiad, Bronze medalist on Board 1
  • 1981 - Asian Chess Team Championships, Gold Medal on Board 1
  • 1983 - Asian Chess Team Championships, Gold Medal on Board 1
  • 1984 - 4th International Bugojno Tournament, 3rd place
  • 2002 - Philippine National Champion
  • 2005 - Southeast Asian Games, Bronze Medal
  • 2014 - National Chess Championships – Battle of GMs, Champion
“Thank you Eugene for being one of the few Filipino Chessers who never had a bad word to say about me.
-- Grandmaster Wesley So
